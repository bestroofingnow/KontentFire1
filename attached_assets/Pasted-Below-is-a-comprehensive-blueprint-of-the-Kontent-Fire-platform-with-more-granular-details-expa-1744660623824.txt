# **Kontent Fire: Comprehensive Plan**

## 1. **Product Name & Branding**

### 1.1 Name & Theme
- **Product Name**: **Kontent Fire**  
- **Subscription Plans**:  
  - **Blaze** (\$99/month)  
  - **Inferno** (premium price set by Admin)  

### 1.2 Brand Identity
- **Design Language**: Bold, dynamic, “fiery” style reflecting energy and momentum.  
- **UI/UX Goal**: Even though the theme is “fire,” the interface should remain sleek, clean, and highly intuitive for a wide range of users (from solopreneurs to marketing teams).  

---

## 2. **High-Level Product Overview**

Kontent Fire is an all-in-one AI-powered content generation and publishing platform that helps businesses:

1. **Generate** text (blogs, social media posts), images, and videos through AI.  
2. **Refine** text to human-like quality with advanced AI language models.  
3. **Schedule** and **publish** content to multiple social platforms automatically or manually.  
4. **Manage** their online presence through a single, unified dashboard.

**Key Differentiator**: The platform integrates multiple AI services (OpenAI, Claude, Perplexity, Runway) to provide a complete suite of content generation (text, image, video) and advanced scheduling features.

---

## 3. **Detailed Feature Breakdown**

### 3.1 AI Content Creation

1. **Text Generation Pipeline**  
   1. **Prompt Creation**: User provides a brief, or chooses from predefined templates (e.g., product promotion, seasonal greeting, industry news).  
   2. **Initial Draft via OpenAI**:  
      - GPT-based model generates a first draft.  
      - Users can specify tone, audience, style (casual, formal, technical, etc.).  
   3. **Refinement with Claude**:  
      - Draft is sent to Claude to enhance natural flow, clarity, and human-like tone.  
      - Content is returned to the user for review.  
   4. **Fact-Checking & References with Perplexity (Optional)**:  
      - If enabled, system looks for reputable, non-competitive sources.  
      - Helps ensure factual accuracy and relevant citations.  
   5. **Manual Edits by User**:  
      - WYSIWYG editor to finalize content before scheduling or publishing.

2. **Image Generation**  
   1. **OpenAI DALL·E (or similar)**:  
      - Generate images based on prompts (“A modern living room with bright pastel colors,” etc.).  
      - Users can adjust style (photorealistic, cartoon, abstract).  
   2. **Image Library**:  
      - All generated images stored in the user’s library.  
      - Tagging and sorting for easy retrieval and re-use.  

3. **Video Creation**  
   1. **Runway Integration**:  
      - Users can produce short AI-generated clips.  
      - Potential use-cases: product intros, quick brand promos, behind-the-scenes montages.  
   2. **Basic Video Editor**:  
      - Add subtitles, transitions, or overlays.  
      - AI-generated text can be auto-injected into videos (e.g., captions or lower thirds).

### 3.2 Scheduling & Publishing

1. **Manual Scheduling** (Blaze):
   - Users pick exact dates/times manually for each social post or blog.  
   - Ideal for those who prefer hands-on control.

2. **Automatic Scheduling** (Inferno):
   - Users define content volume/frequency (e.g., 3 posts/week).  
   - Kontent Fire automatically generates (text & images) and schedules posts.  
   - Optional “Review before Posting” feature to ensure user can edit auto-generated content if desired.

3. **Platform Integrations**:
   - **Major Social Platforms**: Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest.  
   - **Blogs/Website**: WordPress (REST API), potentially Wix, Squarespace, etc.  
     - Different length options for blog posts (short, medium, long).  
     - Post scheduling in alignment with website’s CMS capabilities.

4. **Self-Promoting Feature**:
   - Kontent Fire can generate its own promotional posts (e.g., new features, platform announcements).  
   - Admin can toggle or customize these posts.

### 3.3 Analytics & Performance Tracking
1. **Basic Analytics** (Both Plans):
   - Track likes, comments, shares for each post.  
   - Simple overview of blog traffic (if possible via integrated CMS API).
2. **Advanced Analytics** (Potential Add-On for Inferno):
   - Funnel analysis: click-through rates, conversions (if properly integrated with user’s website).  
   - In-depth time-of-day posting analysis.

## 5. **Master Admin Portal**

### 5.1 Admin Capabilities

1. **User & Account Management**  
   - View all subscribers, usage stats (AI calls, generated content, etc.), subscription status, and payment history.  
   - Ability to upgrade/downgrade user plans or cancel subscriptions manually.

2. **Feature Management**  
   - Turn specific features on or off for each plan (e.g., enable advanced analytics for Inferno only).  
   - Configure maximum usage limits (e.g., capping the number of AI calls for cost control if needed).

3. **Pricing Management**  
   - Blaze plan is fixed at \$99/month but can be changed by admin if needed.  
   - Inferno price is fully customizable.  
   - Admin can define special discounts, promo codes, or trials.

4. **Billing & Payment Integration**  
   - Connect to payment processor (Stripe, PayPal, etc.).  
   - Manage subscription renewals, refunds, invoice generation, etc.

5. **System Analytics**  
   - Summaries of AI usage (calls to OpenAI, Claude, etc.), platform activity (posts published, images generated).  
   - Financial metrics: monthly recurring revenue (MRR), user churn, user acquisition.

6. **Platform-Wide Settings**  
   - Toggle “Self-Promotion” on or off, define intervals for auto-generated promotional posts about Kontent Fire.  
   - Manage internal API keys (OpenAI, Claude, Perplexity, Runway).  
   - Security settings (enforce 2FA, password policies, etc.).

## 6. **Technical Architecture & Considerations**

### 6.1 Frontend
- **Framework**: React/Vue/Angular for a responsive single-page application (SPA).  
- **UI/UX**:  
  - Drag-and-drop interface for scheduling if possible (calendar view).  
  - Real-time previews of AI-generated text and images.

### 6.2 Backend
- **Core Services**:  
  - Node.js, Python (Django, Flask, FastAPI), or similar.  
  - Microservices approach (optional) for each major function (AI text generation, scheduling, user management) to help scale easily.
- **API Integrations**:  
  - **OpenAI** for text/image.  
  - **Claude** for content refinement.  
  - **Perplexity** for references/fact-checking.  
  - **Runway** for AI video generation.  
  - **Social Platforms** (OAuth 2.0 or each platform’s official API).  
  - **WordPress or Other CMS** REST APIs.
- **Scheduling Mechanism**:  
  - Background jobs (e.g., Celery for Python, Bull for Node.js) or cron-like system for queued/scheduled posts.  
  - Must ensure reliability & handle re-tries in case of API downtime.

### 6.3 Database & Data Management
- **Choice of Database**:  
  - Relational (PostgreSQL) or NoSQL (MongoDB) based on developer preference.  
  - Store user info, subscription details, usage logs, generated content, scheduling data.  
- **Caching**:  
  - Potential in-memory caching (Redis) for quick retrieval of recent AI content or scheduling tasks.  

### 6.4 Security & Compliance
- **User Authentication**:  
  - Secure login (JWT or session-based).  
  - OAuth 2.0 for social media account linking.  
- **API Key Management**:  
  - Store third-party API keys securely (e.g., encrypted in the database or vault solution).  
- **Data Protection**:  
  - If operating in regions with GDPR/CCPA, ensure compliance (deletion requests, data portability, etc.).
- **Monitoring & Logging**:  
  - Collect logs for system events, user actions, errors.  
  - Real-time alerts for critical failures (API rate limits, scheduling issues, etc.).

## 7. **User Experience & Workflow**

1. **Registration / Onboarding**  
   - User lands on a pricing page (Blaze vs. Inferno).  
   - After sign-up/payment, they’re guided to connect social media accounts (via OAuth) and possibly WordPress (if they have a blog).  
   - Interactive AI tutorial walks them through generating their first text/image post.

2. **Content Generation & Editing**  
   - **Prompt Input**: Users type a description or goal of their post.  
   - **Real-Time Preview**: Watch as the content is generated (text + any images).  
   - **Editing**: Modify text or images, add final touches.

3. **Scheduling**  
   - **Blaze**: Manual “pick a date/time” approach for each piece of content.  
   - **Inferno**: Automatic scheduling (define “3 posts/week”), plus manual override if desired.

4. **Publishing & Analytics**  
   - Published content flows to each connected platform.  
   - Engagement metrics returned to Kontent Fire for tracking.  
   - Users see post-by-post data, monthly overviews, etc.

5. **Plan Upgrade/Downgrade**  
   - From Blaze, user can move to Inferno if they want auto-posting/video features.  
   - Upgrade triggers immediate access to premium features (depending on billing cycle).

## 9. **Additional Considerations**

1. **Content Moderation & Prompt Filtering**  
   - Ensure open-text prompts don’t produce harmful, copyrighted, or offensive content inadvertently (use OpenAI’s content filters, if available).  
2. **Cost Management**  
   - Monitor AI usage costs, especially with unlimited text/image generation.  
   - Consider introducing “fair use” policies or usage caps to avoid unexpected bills.  
3. **Customer Support**  
   - Blaze: standard email support.  
   - Inferno: priority chat or phone support.  
4. **Marketing & Growth**  
   - Offer a free trial or limited-time discount to attract new users.  
   - Build an affiliate program or referral incentives.
